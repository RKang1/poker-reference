Post-Flop Strategy (Practical, Rake-Aware)

Default line: value bet thinner than you bluff, especially versus players who call too wide and raise too little.

SpotBaseline Plan
SRP as preflop raiser (IP)C-bet 25-33% often on dry A/K-high boards. Check more on wet/connected boards that hit caller range.
SRP as preflop raiser (OOP)Use more checks. Bet stronger value and best draws; avoid auto c-betting marginal one-pair hands on bad textures.
Single-raised pot sizingSmall size with range advantage; 60-75% with strong value and high-equity draws on dynamic boards.
Facing flop stabContinue with pair+draw, good overcards+backdoors, and strong draws. Fold weak overcards with no backdoor equity.
Turn playBarrel cards that improve your value/bluffs (overcards, flush/straight cards for your range). Slow down on bricks when called by sticky ranges.
River decisionsValue bet hands that get called by worse. Bluff mostly with blockers to villain's strongest bluff-catchers and missed draws.
3-bet pots (IP)C-bet small frequently on high-card boards. On low/wet boards, check more and realize equity with medium strength hands.
3-bet pots (OOP)Play tighter: check-call with medium strength, check-raise with strong value + best draws, avoid low-equity bluffs.
Population exploit (small stakes)Under-bluffed rivers and over-called flops/turns are common. Bluff less on river, value bet more aggressively.
Red-line controlDo not force bluffs just to win every pot. Prioritize showdowns with medium strength in rake-heavy games.
